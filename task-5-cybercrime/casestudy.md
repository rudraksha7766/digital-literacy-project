**The Crime:** UPI fraud occurs when a person is tricked by a cybercriminal into sending money from their bank accounts.

**How It Happens:** A typical case of UPI fraud might begin with a college student selling a used textbook or a bicycle
on a website where people buy and sell used items. The scammer contacts the student and agrees on the price 
of the book or bicycle immediately. The scammer then pretends that in order to gain trust, 
they will be sending the money for the book or bicycle through a UPI app, for example, 
Google Pay or PhonePe. The scammer then sends a 'Collect Request' or a QR code to the student, 
stating that they need to enter the UPI PIN in order to collect the payment.

**Who Is Targeted:** College students are commonly targeted in UPI fraud because they are highly involved 
in buying and selling second-hand items, are highly active on UPI apps, and are in a 
rush due to attending college.

**The Consequences:** When the student enters their UPI PIN under the belief that they are receiving money,
the money is actually being deducted from their account and transferred to the scammer's account.
This leads to a loss for the student, which can be extremely detrimental for a student living on a budget, 
as well as a high level of stress and a violation of their personal financial security.
